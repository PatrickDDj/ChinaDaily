The number of TEUs transported via the land-sea freight routes totaled 183,200 in the period, a year-on-year increase of 109 percent, according to the port and logistics office of Chongqing. The land-sea freight routes are part of the New International Land-Sea Trade Corridor, a trade and logistics passage jointly built by western Chinese regions and Southeast Asian countries. To promote the construction of the corridor, Chongqing has built a convenient transportation hub system linking it with its surrounding cities.
